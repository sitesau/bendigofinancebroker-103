Bendigo Finance Broker Comparison Explained

When seeking funding for business growth or equipment, the choice between approaching a single bank directly or engaging a bendigo finance broker significantly impacts your options. A direct application accepts the policy and rate of one institution. In contrast, a broker compares rates, fees and policy fit across a panel of more than 30 lenders. This broad search is particularly valuable for regional businesses, as a specific lender's policy might decline a newer business, while another on the panel might approve it.

Cost is a key differentiator in this comparison. Going direct costs nothing but offers no comparison. Using a broker is typically also free to the borrower, as the lender pays the broker a commission upon settlement. This structure allows the broker to negotiate competitive rates through volume without charging the business owner a fee. For businesses in sectors like agriculture or manufacturing, this means turning a single application into a comprehensive market review without incurring extra costs.

Chattel Mortgage Versus Finance Lease

The selection of a finance structure determines the tax treatment and ownership of the asset. The two primary options compared by brokers are chattel mortgages and finance leases. A chattel mortgage functions like a secured loan where the business owns the asset immediately. This structure allows for the claiming of GST upfront on the purchase price and claiming interest deductions on the loan repayments.

A finance lease differs significantly; the lender retains ownership during the term, and the business makes lease payments. Under a lease, GST is charged on the individual repayments rather than the full asset value upfront. The decision impacts cash flow and tax position, so reviewing the difference between depreciation deductions versus potential deductible repayments is necessary. The end of term also varies, with chattel mortgages offering an optional balloon payment to finalise the loan, while leases typically require the asset to be returned or purchased.

Asset and Working Capital Options

Finance products differ depending on whether the target is a physical asset or cash flow. Equipment and asset finance cover tangible items such as trucks, machinery, manufacturing equipment and medical technology. For physical assets, the item itself often serves as security, which simplifies approval compared to unsecured loans. When comparing new versus used assets, most lenders finance both, provided the equipment meets age criteria. For example, trucks generally need to be under 15 years old at the end of the loan term.

In comparison, working capital solutions or business loans fund intangible growth rather than specific hardware. These options, ranging from 20,000 dollars to over 500,000 dollars, require a stronger focus on the business's trading history and cash flow serviceability rather than the value of a specific machine. This distinction is crucial for businesses deciding whether to refinance existing debt or fund new expansion.

Approval Speed and Process

The timeline for securing finance varies depending on the complexity of the application and the asset type. A standard application through a broker can achieve pre-approval within 24 hours. This speed provides certainty when negotiating asset purchases. Full approval and settlement generally take between 2 and 5 business days once all documents are submitted.

Equipment finance tends to settle faster than commercial property loans because the asset being purchased provides clear security. Complex structures or used assets requiring independent valuation may extend the timeframe, whereas straightforward unsecured business loans for smaller amounts can sometimes offer same-day approval. The documentation phase involves supplying financials, bank statements and the asset quote, which a broker manages to ensure the application meets the specific lender's policy requirements.

Common questions

Is using a finance broker free? For most business borrowers, yes. The lender pays the broker a commission on settlement, so the service is typically free to you, and any fee must be disclosed before you proceed.

What is the main difference between a chattel mortgage and a finance lease? With a chattel mortgage, you own the asset from day one and claim GST upfront. With a finance lease, the lender owns the asset and you lease it, with GST charged on the repayments.

Can I finance used equipment? Yes, both new and used equipment can typically be financed. However, age limits often apply, such as trucks needing to be under 15 years old at the end of the loan term.
